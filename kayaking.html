places to do kayaking in India

The sooner you paddle, the better you explore.’ Irrespective of what kind of a traveler you are, a touch of adventure is something that you will definitely embrace, especially if it takes you right into the lap of nature. Kayaking in India is one such experience that can give you the much-needed thrill and make your holiday one-of-its-kind. Be it a lake, a river, or a sea, paddling onto an off beaten path amidst the wonders of nature lets you have the best of both worlds. But which are the best destinations for kayaking in India and what’s the best time to plan this adventure are surely some of the questions that may pop in your head. But fret not, this handy little guide has got you covered! From kayaking boat price in India to the top tips that you must keep in mind before heading out for the experience, this guide has got it all. So, just take a back seat, and start scrolling below!

1. Zanskar River, Leh

Zanskar River is one of the best places that offer the most adventurous experiences for kayaking and canoeing activities in India. The trip starts on the Indus River from Spituk and goes on until you reach Nimu. Passing through various rapids of grade 1 to 5, the ultimate thrill is what you’d get to taste while paddling there. So, if you have a good amount of time for a thrilling holiday, then think no more and just head out!

Location: Zanskar, Leh, India

Best time to visit: April to June

2. Nagin Lake, Srinagar

This lake is quite a pretty spot for kayaking in Kashmir. Located on the east side of the city, this is a much calmer place to relax than the famous Dal Lake. The crystal clear, blue waters reflect the eternal beauty of the greenery that environs the lake and is a pleasant sight to gaze at. No doubt, this is a great place to paddle at your own pace. And when you’d done kayaking, you can peacefully end the day with a houseboat stay!

Location: Lake, Srinagar, India

Best time to visit: April to October

3. The Ganges, Rishikesh

Rishikesh is yet another kickass destination for kayaking in India. The 12-km expedition begins at Brahmapuri and ends at Rishikesh’s popular ghat. The heavier rapids that you’d come across would surely pump up your adrenaline rush and keep you energized for the whole journey. What makes this place special is that it offers much more to sight than jus nature. The ashrams and temples oozing bliss are amongst the top places to spot on your way there. Kayaking in Rishikesh is an absolutely thrilling experience.

Location: Brahmapuri, Rishikesh, India

Best time to visit: March to early-May (September to mid-November for rafting)

4. Mandovi, Goa

Be it the gorgeous beaches, the spectacular backwaters, or the stunning rivers of Goa, this is one of the most popular places to experience kayaking in India. The extraordinary experience is worth a tick off your bucket list and offers much more thrill than partying at Cubana. While paddling across Mandovi or Nerul, you can also witness the soothing beauty of the sunset besides the laid-back charm of the town.

Location: Mandovi River, Panjim, India

Best time to visit: November to March

5. Teesta River, Sikkim

Of all the places for kayaking in India, Teesta River in Sikkim is a famous hotspot that is gradually gaining popularity. If you’re one of those travelers who like to skip the crowd and embrace calmness, then this is the perfect place for you to learn and experience the adventure. Spectacular mountains and rapids are a few things that would add stars to your overall experience.

Location: Teesta River, East Sikkim, India

Best time to visit: April to June

6. Havelock Island, Andaman

For those of you who don’t believe in rowing across a lake, the sea of Andaman is the ocean. You can plan a proper trip to here for experiencing kayaking in India, while stopping for village tours, tasting toothsome cuisines, and go island hopping. With a varied range of marine species swimming around you, you surely won’t feel alone in the sea while paddling your way through.

Location: Havelock Island, Andaman Islands, India

Best time to visit:December to February

7. Paradise Beach, Pondicherry

Famous all across the country for its everlasting beauty, white sand, and paradisiacal views, the Paradise Beach in Pondicherry offers a perfect experience of kayaking in India. Unfortunately, you cannot indulge in the thrilling experience all the time here as the beach stays open only from 9 am to 5 pm and allows all water based sports and activities after considering the nature of the sea and the wind each day.

Location: Manaveli Road, Chinna Veerampattinam, Puducherry, India

Best time to visit: October to March
